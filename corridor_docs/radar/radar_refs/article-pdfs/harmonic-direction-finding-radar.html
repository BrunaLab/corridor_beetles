Harmonic Direction Finding Radar

Tracking Juvenile Wood Frogs With Harmonic Radar (2010 – 2013)

 

Wood frogs are a source of nutrients for other wildlife, hence their health and survival determines the health of the forest. Juvenile frogs have a length of less then 4 centimeters and a weight of approximately 0.6 grams, making battery powered tag applications impractical. A passive tag using a Schottky diode was developed, along with a 5.8 GHz / 11.6 GHz harmonic direction finding radar, to track juvenile wood frogs in the field.

This research was been funded by the National Science Foundation.

  • Faculty: Nuri Emanetoglu (ECE), Herbert Aumann (ECE), Malcolm Hunter (WLE)
  • Graduate students: Brittany Cline
  • Undergraduate students: Evan Kus, Matthew Krenik, Margaret Thompson
